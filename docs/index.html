1 Project Overview

As an avid chess player, I’m always eager to explore new strategies and refine my skills. I play mainly on chess.com, which is the most popular chess website in the world. I usually play games against human opponents, and I like to play with the settings that each player has 10 minutes to make all their moves.

My goal for this project was to determine if I could find any insights from other players’ games that could help me win more often. To do this, I needed to get a large sample of completed chess.com games, engineer features that could potentially explain why someone won their game, and lastly run a model to determine which features were significant and interpret them.

1.1 Methodology

Using the chess.com API, I was able to extract a random sample of 5,800 completed 10-minute chess games from players around the world at my skill level (1000).

No player was represented more than one time, and no games that ended as a draw was kept because I wanted to analyze games that ended decisively.
